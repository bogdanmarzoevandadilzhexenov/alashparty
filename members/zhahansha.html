Zhakhansha Dosmukhamedov (1887-1938) (Kazakh: Жаһанша Досмұхамедов) is a Kazakh public activist, lawyer, one of the leaders of the Alash party.

Brief information
Early life
Political life



Zhakhansha was born in 1887 in the family of a steppe cattle breeder Dosmukhamed from the Kazakh family of Karakunan Tana. Russian Russian-Kazakh father arranged for Zhakhansha to attend the Buldurta aul mobile Russian-Kazakh school, after which Zhakhansha entered the Russian-Kazakh school in the village of Dzhambeyty. Considering Zhakhanshi's abilities, the school's management recommended sending him to the Ural Military Real School. The volost administrator, an honorary citizen of the Russian Empire Mukhamedzhan Sarykudzhin, helped Dosmukhamedov to get into this educational institution.

Zhakhansha was first enrolled in the preparatory class of the military real school in Uralsk, then entered the first year. There were 300 students in one preparatory, six main and one additional classes of the school, including 18 Kazakh children, whose scholarships were paid by Kazakh elders - stewards of steppe volosts. Among these 18 students are Zhakhansha and his future ally in the Alash party, Khalel Dosmukhamedov. Thanks to his excellent memory, Zhakhansha showed knowledge in mathematics, physics, chemistry and biology. During his college years, he went from class to class without exams.




In 1917, he was chairman of the Ural Regional Kazakh Committee, a participant in the All-Russian Congress of Muslims in Moscow, at which he was elected deputy chairman of the All-Russian Council of Muslims. Participated in the preparation of the draft law on the Constituent Assembly of the Provisional Government. He became a member of the government of Alash-Orda, proclaimed at the 2nd All-Kazakh Congress in December 1917. In 1918-1919 he headed the Western Branch of Alash-Orda. In March 1918 , together with Khalel Dosmukhamedov. participated in negotiations with V. I. Lenin and I. V. Stalin.

He made a great contribution to the development of Kazakh national culture, science and education. In 1922-1925 he was a member of the Society for the development of culture "Talap". Translated the civil and criminal codes into Kazakh. "Criminal Code" ("Zhauyzdyk nizamnamasy") translated by Dosmukhamedov, it was published in 1000 copies. Dosmukhamedov was arrested several times in the 1930s. He spent the last year of his life in Moscow, being half paralyzed after a stroke. In 1938 he was arrested as an "enemy of the people". He was shot on August 3, 1938 at the Butovo training ground, where he was buried. Rehabilitated on December 2, 1957.
