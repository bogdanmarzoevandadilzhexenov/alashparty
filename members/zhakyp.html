Zhakyp Akpayev (1876-1934) (Kazakh: Жақып Ақбаев) — Kazakh Russian and Soviet public figure, Kazakh nobleman, jurist and ethnographer; at the beginning of the XX century — also a politician, member of the Alashorda government. I wrote a number of works on Kazakh ethnography and everyday life. He lives from childhood in Kary-kanly from Kanly.

Brief information
Early life
Political life



He was born on October 25, 1876 in Karkaraly uyezd, Semipalatinsk region, Berikkara Parish, in the village of No. 3 in a wealthy family. The argyn tribe comes from the yellow Tanas branch of the Karakesek tribe. In 1886, he studied at the Kazakh boarding school in Karkaraly, in 1889 he entered the Omsk gymnasium, where he studied for 7 years, then transferred to the Tomsk gymnasium, where he studied for a year, graduated in 1898 and entered the Faculty of law of St. Petersburg University. In 1903, he graduated with a diploma of the 1st degree. When studying at a university, it is necessary to master various branches of knowledge.

In order to fully master the ability of human memory and methods of weighing facts, he also writes an application to the heads of the university to listen to a lecture from Professor of mnemonics S. Feinstein in Odessa and apply for admission to the Institute of Archaeology in St. Petersburg. In the state testing Commission, the topic "Marriage Law of Kazakhs" is approved for defense. Later, while in prison, he continued and expanded on this topic and published it in 1907 in the notes of the Semipalatinsk branch of the RGS. After graduating from the University, he joined the Omsk judicial chamber. In August 1905, there was a conciliatory Court of the 2nd Precinct in Omsk.




During the Revolution of 1905-1907, he took part in the revolutionary events in Omsk, Pavlodar and Karkaralinsk, including on July 22, 1905, he signed an appeal of Kazakh intellectuals to the tsar and on November 15-16 of the same year he made anti-Russian speeches in Karkaralinsk. For supporting the revolution, he was subjected to political repression by the tsarist government. Since 1917, he was the Kazakh representative in "Shura-i-Islami". He was an active member of the Alash Party. Since December 1917, he was a member of the government of Alash-Orda. He participated in both All-Kazakh congresses of 1917, held after the February Revolution. In 1917, the Kazakh committee of the Karkarala district opened a "Yakub Madrasah" (Zhakyp) in his honor with free tuition.

In the same year, 1917, he was persecuted by the authorities of the Provisional Government, in 1918-1919 — by the government of Kolchak, and in the last years of his life - by the Soviet authorities, although for a short time he served in the court of the People's Commissar in Semipalatinsk and in the department of public education of the Karkaralinsky district.

He was first arrested on January 11, 1906 on charges of high treason. Then in 1908, 1910 and 1929, for his beliefs, he was imprisoned in Karkaralinsk, Pavlodar, Semipalatinsk, Omsk, Alma-Ata. He was in exile several times, including in Yakutia and the Semirechensk region. From the last exile to Voronezh, where he was exiled in 1929, he returned in 1934 seriously ill and soon died. He was fully rehabilitated by the decision of the Supreme Court of the Kazakh SSR on February 28, 1958, but his relatives became aware of it only in 1990.
